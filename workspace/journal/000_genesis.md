## 1. The Primitive Mathematics

### 1.1 The Golden Ratio (φ)

φ = (1 + √5) / 2 ≈ 1.6180339887498948482

The golden ratio is the fundamental growth constant of the protocol.  An
organism at generation *n* operates at scale φⁿ relative to its base.
Categories are partitioned in the ratio φ:1.  Relevance decays by φ⁻¹ per
generation.  The golden ratio is not chosen — it is derived from the
requirement that growth be self-similar at every scale.

**Property:** φ² = φ + 1.  This means every generation contains within it
the sum of the previous two — the same structural recursion as the Fibonacci
sequence.

### 1.2 The Fibonacci Sequence

F(n) = F(n-1) + F(n-2), with F(0) = 0, F(1) = 1

The Fibonacci sequence governs temporal growth.  Organisms advance
generations at Fibonacci number thresholds of their accumulated state
(documents ingested, organisms spawned, nodes registered).  This creates a
natural deceleration of structural change — early growth is rapid, later
growth is deliberate — matching the maturation pattern of biological
organisms.

**Binet's Formula:** F(n) = (φⁿ − ψⁿ) / √5, where ψ = (1 − √5)/2

This closed-form expression expresses every Fibonacci number as a direct
function of the golden ratio, confirming that Fibonacci and φ are two faces
of the same mathematical identity.

### 1.3 The Golden Angle (≈ 137.508°)

θ = 2π(2 − φ) ≈ 2.3999632 radians

The golden angle governs spatial distribution.  When organisms, nodes, or
seeds are placed at successive multiples of the golden angle, they achieve
the most uniform distribution possible without any two elements overlapping
or clustering.  This is phyllotaxis — the same pattern sunflowers use to
arrange seeds, pine cones use to arrange scales, and galaxies use to arrange
spiral arms.

### 1.4 Zeckendorf's Theorem

Every positive integer has a unique representation as a sum of non-
consecutive Fibonacci numbers.  This decomposition is the organism's native
number system — any quantity is expressed not in base-10 or binary, but as a
sum of Fibonacci components.  This ensures that the organism's arithmetic
preserves golden-ratio relationships at every level.
